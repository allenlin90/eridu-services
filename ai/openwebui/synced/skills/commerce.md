# Commerce

---

## General

### What Commerce does

Commerce is ERIDU's client-facing pillar and e-commerce enablement engine. It lands clients, manages accounts, delivers shop enablement services, and coordinates Erify and ERISA when multi-pillar delivery is needed. Commerce owns the client relationship across all pillars.

**Teams:**
- Client Success team — client acquisition (sales pipeline), account management, client relationships
- Enablement team — operates and optimizes client shops, delivers growth roadmaps, co-works with Erify and ERISA for overall shop performance
- Product person — dedicated role managing vendor/product sourcing across all pillars via Product CRM

**Owned by:** Head of Commerce

### Revenue models

- Flat fees from shop enablement services
- % on GMV from shops managed
- Marketing service fees from existing client accounts (upsell/cross-sell)

### Cost structure

- Primary cost: account management and enablement team headcount per shop managed
- Margin drivers: enablement fee vs cost per shop, GMV performance (% take), marketing service revenue from existing accounts, client retention and upsell rate

### Client tiering

| Tier | Definition |
|---|---|
| SA (Strategic Account) | Highest-value clients — premium service, senior account ownership |
| KA (Key Account) | High-value clients — dedicated account management |
| FA (Filler Account) | Lower-value accounts — standardized service, lighter touch |

Tier determines service level, account management intensity, and escalation priority.

## Account management

### Core responsibilities

- Achieving and maximizing GMV performance across all managed accounts — this is the primary objective
- Billing and collection process — ensure timely invoicing and payment follow-up
- Record and maintain full client profile and activities in CRM (Northstar Sales CRM)
- Contracts and documents stored in Google Drive
- Maximize MOT (Moment of Truth) — every client interaction should reinforce a positive impression of ERIDU
- Maintain response time, quality, and consistency standards (see governance-ops.md for response time SLAs)

Key onboarding steps include:

- Onboarding meeting — formal kickoff with client
- Alignment on expectations: service scope, SLA, GMV targets, communication cadence and channels
- Brand guardrails install — client's brand guidelines (logo, tone, do's and don'ts) documented and installed into operations across all teams involved in delivery

- Every client account must have brand guardrails documented before production begins
- Guardrails include: brand identity guidelines, approved/prohibited messaging, product positioning, platform-specific rules
- Guardrails are shared with Erify (for livestream LoS) and ERISA (for creator content) as part of cross-pillar handoff
- Brand brief and LoS must be approved and confirmed by account before any production is scheduled

### Market intelligence

- Account managers update market intelligence as part of ongoing account management
- Includes: competitor activity, platform trends, category performance, client industry developments
- Used to inform growth roadmap adjustments and upsell/cross-sell opportunities

### Reporting duties

- Regular client reporting on performance against GMV targets and growth roadmap
- Reporting cadence aligned with client agreement (typically weekly or monthly)
- Internal reporting to Head of Commerce on account health, pipeline, and revenue

## Enablement operations

### Growth roadmap

When engaging a brand client, the Enablement team analyzes the account and devises a 12-month growth roadmap:
- GMV target and strategies
- Growth driver mix based on the four key GMV drivers in content commerce:
  - **Livestream** — live selling sessions (delivered by Erify)
  - **Short video** — content-driven product promotion (owned by Erify, temporarily outsourced to ERISA creators)
  - **Affiliate** — creator affiliate campaigns (delivered by ERISA)
  - **Shopping center** — shop tab optimization and management
- Performance incentives tied to roadmap milestones

The growth roadmap serves as the main blueprint for all operations on the account.

### Enablement review cadence

**Daily:**
- Review daily activities and results from each growth driver
- Address operational issues and blockers

**Weekly:**
- Ensure all growth drivers are on track against the roadmap
- Cross-team coordination check

**Monthly:**
- Review performance against GMV targets
- Adjust growth driver mix based on what's working
- Discover new opportunities to grow account GMV
- Discover upsell/cross-sell opportunities for additional services

**Quarterly:**
- Review performance against roadmap milestones
- Adjust key strategies and targets for next quarter

### Key stats for analysis

- GMV by growth driver (livestream, short video, affiliate, shopping center)
- GMV by product
- GMV trends and growth rate
- Driver mix effectiveness (which drivers are contributing most)
- Account profitability (revenue vs cost of servicing)

### Cross-team ticketing and job requests

- Formal job requests from Commerce to Erify and ERISA for production and creator services
- Currently managed via Google Sheet with LINE message reminders
- In-house ticketing tool being built to replace this process
- Every cross-team request must be logged — no informal requests without a record

### Platform AM meetings

- Routine meetings with platform account managers (TikTok, Shopee, Lazada)
- Purpose: account health reviews, platform program updates, opportunity identification, issue resolution
- Attended by Enablement team and/or Head of Commerce

### Soi Bear Fulfilment

ERIDU's fulfillment service covering:
- Physical product fulfillment (warehousing, shipping)
- Order processing and logistics coordination
- Sample fulfillment for livestreams and campaigns

**Status: highly inactive.** Operations exist but minimal volume. To be scaled when demand justifies.

## Product (management and acquisition)

Product sourcing and vendor management is owned by a dedicated product person under Commerce. All client and vendor products across pillars are managed in the Product CRM (Northstar Product schema).

### Product sourcing process

- **Target:** identify and onboard vendors/products that align with client needs and platform opportunities
- **Source of funnel:** platform recommendations, client requests, market research, competitor analysis, category trends
- **CRM:** all vendor and product records maintained in Northstar Product CRM (product schema)
- **Pitch:** present product opportunities to clients and/or match with ERISA affiliate campaigns

### Vendor criteria

- Must meet quality and compliance standards
- Must offer competitive commercial terms (commission %, incentives, samples)
- Must be able to support content commerce operations (product samples, marketing materials, voucher support)

### Product radar criteria

- High-demand categories on TikTok Shop and Shopee
- Products with strong affiliate commission structures
- Hero products with proven conversion potential
- Seasonal and trending products aligned with campaign calendar

### Product review

- Regular review of product performance across accounts
- Metrics: GMV per product, conversion rate, return rate, commission earned
- Underperforming products flagged for replacement or renegotiation with vendor

## Cross-pillar coordination

**With Erify (Content Management):**
- Commerce provides brand briefs and LoS requirements
- Erify delivers livestream production (standard and premium)
- Brand guardrails must be installed before production begins
- Cross-team job requests logged formally

**With ERISA (Creator Management):**
- Commerce owns client relationships for all client-initiated work
- ERISA operates creator services, paid packages, and affiliate campaigns
- Monthly: ERISA sends top 20 selling brands list to Commerce for ADP opportunities
- Commerce sells ADP (max 5 launches/month, CM approval required), ERISA operates
- Commerce sells AMP (affiliate enablement retainer), ERISA operates the strategy

**Coordination rules:**
- Commerce / Client Success is always the client relationship owner, even when Erify or ERISA leads delivery
- Exception: standardized livestream services → Erify leads delivery, Commerce remains relationship owner
- Exception: percentage-only affiliate campaigns → ERISA leads delivery, Commerce remains relationship owner

## Key metrics, SLAs, and operational cadence

### Key metrics

**Account management:**
- Client retention / churn rate
- Client satisfaction (MOT quality)
- Average deal value
- Upsell/cross-sell revenue from existing accounts
- Billing collection rate and aging

**Enablement:**
- Total GMV managed across all accounts
- GMV by growth driver (livestream, short video, affiliate, shopping center)
- Growth roadmap milestone achievement rate
- Account profitability

**Product:**
- Number of active vendors
- Product GMV contribution
- New vendor onboarding rate

### SLAs

- Client response time: within 1 hour on LINE, within 24 hours on email (per governance-ops.md)
- Brand guardrails installed before any production begins
- Growth roadmap delivered within onboarding period for every new client
- Cross-team job requests must be formally logged
- Client payment overdue beyond 7 days triggers escalation (per financial-guardrails.md)

### Operational cadence

**Daily:**
- Enablement: review activities and results from each growth driver per account
- Client Success: pipeline management, client communications

**Weekly:**
- Enablement: growth driver tracking — are things on track?
- Client account reviews: delivery, GMV, satisfaction
- Business review: Head of Commerce meets with Country Manager
- Platform AM meetings as scheduled

**Monthly:**
- Enablement: performance review and growth driver mix adjustment per account
- Upsell/cross-sell opportunity identification
- Receive top 20 selling brands list from ERISA for ADP opportunities
- Product review: vendor and product performance
- Pillar P&L review: Head of Commerce + Country Manager

**Quarterly:**
- QBR: Commerce pillar → Country Manager → Founder
- Growth roadmap strategy adjustments per account
- Client tier review (SA/KA/FA reassignment if warranted)
- Platform partnership review

**Annually:**
- Client growth strategy and target setting
- Account portfolio review and tier restructuring
- Product sourcing strategy
