# HR Operations

---

## General

### What HR covers

HR is an operational support function serving all three pillars and company-wide. It handles recruiting, onboarding, payroll preparation, employee records, compliance with Thai labor law, and access management.

**Team:** Dedicated internal HR person

**Reports to:** Country Manager

**Key legal framework:** Thai Labor Protection Act B.E. 2541 (1998) and amendments including LPA Amendment No. 9 B.E. 2568 (2025). Companies with 10+ employees must maintain written work rules in Thai, displayed at the workplace and submitted to the local labor office.

---

## Org chart

- Founder → Country Manager → Heads of Pillars (Head of Content, Head of Creator, Head of Commerce) → Team Leads → Individual contributors
- Operational support: Finance, HR
- Three pillars: Commerce (Client Success + Enablement + Product), Erify (Offset + Onset), ERISA (Recruit + Creator Services + Campaign)
- ~50 people total: 20–25 full-time, 10–15 part-time, 20–40 contractors
- Org chart maintained by HR, reviewed quarterly, updated when roles change

## HR policies and guidelines

### Employment types and records

**Employment types at ERIDU:**

| Type | Contract | Social Security | Withholding tax | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Full-time employee | Employment contract | SSO registered (Section 33) | PND 1 (payroll WHT) | Standard labor law protections apply |
| Part-time employee | Employment contract (part-time terms) | SSO registered if eligible | PND 1 | Same labor protections, pro-rated benefits |
| Contractor (MC, creator, freelancer) | Service contract | Not SSO registered | PND 3 (individual) | Not covered by Labor Protection Act — governed by contract terms |
| Intern | Internship agreement | Not SSO registered | PND 1 if paid | Limited scope, HR recruits and decides |

**Record keeping:**
- Employment contracts must be in writing (Thai language recommended for compliance)
- All contracts, documents, and amendments stored in employee file (Google Drive, restricted access)
- Employee register maintained in Thai with payment records, overtime, holidays — retained minimum 2 years after termination (per LPA requirement)
- Contractor contracts and documents must be fully collected and stored before onboarding (especially MCs — see content-management.md)

### Onboarding

**Onboarding checklist:**
- Signed employment/service contract
- Copy of national ID and tax documents
- SSO registration (employees only — within 30 days of start date per Social Security Act)
- Bank account details for payroll
- Emergency contact information
- System access provisioned (see Access Management)
- Employee handbook acknowledged and signed
- Brand guardrails and confidentiality briefing
- Introduction to team, tools, and workflows
- Assigned onboarding buddy or manager

**Probation:**
- Contract probation: 119 days (Thai law standard — avoids severance obligation at 120 days)
- Internal management evaluation: 90 days
- Probationary employees are still protected by the Labor Protection Act

**Probation evaluation process:**
- Month 1 (Day 30): first check-in — manager reviews initial task performance, cultural fit, attendance. Documented feedback provided.
- Month 2 (Day 60): mid-probation review — manager evaluates progress against assigned tasks and objectives. Issues flagged early for course correction.
- Month 3 (Day 90): final evaluation — formal pass/fail decision by manager.

**Pass criteria (all must be met):**
- Assigned tasks and deliverables completed to acceptable quality
- Demonstrates understanding of role responsibilities and pillar operations
- Meets attendance and punctuality standards
- Follows SOPs and company protocols
- Positive working relationship with team
- No unresolved performance or conduct issues

**Outcomes at Day 90:**
- Pass: confirmed as permanent employee. Manager documents evaluation.
- Conditional pass: specific areas for improvement identified, probation extended (max to Day 119). Clear goals set.
- Fail: termination before Day 120 (no severance required). Exit process follows offboarding checklist.

**Tracking:** HR tracks all probationary employees monthly and ensures evaluations are completed on schedule. Probation status reviewed in monthly HR calendar.

### Offboarding

**PIP (Performance Improvement Plan) process:**

When an employee's performance is unsatisfactory:

1. **Verbal warning** — manager documents the conversation and specific performance gaps. Sets clear expectations and timeline (14 days).
2. **Written warning** — if no improvement after verbal warning. Formal written notice specifying: performance issues, expected improvements, support offered, timeline (30 days), and consequences of non-improvement. Employee signs acknowledgment. Written warnings are legally important for termination defense.
3. **PIP** — if no improvement after written warning. Formal 30–60 day improvement plan with: specific measurable goals, weekly check-ins, manager support and resources, clear pass/fail criteria.
4. **Termination** — if PIP goals not met. Follows Thai labor law termination requirements (see below).

**Termination requirements (Thai Labor Protection Act):**
- Written notice at least one pay period in advance, or payment in lieu of notice
- Severance pay based on length of service:
  - Under 120 days: no severance
  - 120 days to 1 year: 30 days' wages
  - 1–3 years: 90 days' wages
  - 3–6 years: 180 days' wages
  - 6–10 years: 240 days' wages
  - 10–20 years: 300 days' wages
  - 20+ years: 400 days' wages
- Final pay (salary, overtime, unused annual leave) must be paid within 3 days of termination
- No severance required for termination due to: dishonesty, intentional criminal offense, gross negligence causing serious damage, violation of work rules after written warning (within 1 year), abandonment of duty for 3 consecutive working days, imprisonment

**Offboarding checklist:**
- System access revoked immediately (all systems — see Access Management)
- Company property returned (equipment, keys, ID)
- Final pay calculated and released within 3 days
- SSO termination notification filed within 15 days (Form SorPorSor 6-09)
- Exit interview conducted
- Knowledge transfer completed for critical roles
- Employee removed from LINE groups and shared folders

### Promotion, bonus, and incentives

**Incentives (monthly):**
- Incentive structure is tied to variable cost optimization or revenue growth
- Calculated as a proportion of the value created (e.g., % of cost savings achieved, % of incremental revenue generated)
- Calculated and paid monthly as part of payroll
- Must be measurable and tied to specific KPIs relevant to the employee's role and pillar
- Incentive targets set by Head of Pillar, approved by Country Manager

**Bonus (quarterly):**
- Bonus is tied to one-time success on a project or achieving quarterly strategic goals
- Calculated and assessed quarterly
- Not recurring — each quarter is evaluated independently
- Bonus criteria defined at the start of each quarter as part of goal-setting
- Approved by Head of Pillar + Country Manager

**Promotion:**
- Promotion is tied to consistent demonstration of the skillsets and ownership required at the next level — not just tenure or current role performance
- The employee must show they are already operating at the next level before promotion, not promoted in hopes they will grow into it
- Evaluation criteria: technical/functional skills for the next role, end-to-end ownership of responsibilities, decision-making quality, leadership (if applicable), alignment with ERIDU values
- Promotion decisions made by Head of Pillar, approved by Country Manager, with Founder approval required for Head-level promotions
- Promotions reviewed during quarterly performance discussions, but can be initiated at any time when criteria are clearly met

### Communication rules

- HR communications to employees: via LINE for quick updates, email for formal/documented matters
- Policy changes: communicated in writing via email, acknowledged by employees
- Grievances and complaints: employees can raise issues with direct manager, HR, or Country Manager
- Resolution process: investigate within 7 days, resolve or escalate within 14 days, document outcome
- All HR decisions (warnings, PIP, termination, promotions) must be documented in writing

### Employee handbook

The employee handbook should be maintained as a separate document (Thai language required for legal compliance if 10+ employees). Key contents:

**Culture and values:**
- ERIDU core values: Growth-mindset & Impact-driven, Tech-Forward & Human-Centered, Integrity & Excellence
- Reference core-principles.md "The spirit" section for founder intent

**Working hours:**
- Office staff: fixed hours, 8 hours/day, not exceeding 48 hours/week (per LPA Section 23)
- Studio/production staff (Erify): shift-based, per shift schedule
- Minimum 1 hour rest after 5 consecutive hours of work (per LPA Section 27)
- Overtime: requires prior employee consent (per LPA Section 24), maximum 36 hours/week
- Overtime compensation: 1.5x hourly rate on working days, 2x on holidays, 3x for overtime on holidays

**Leave entitlements (per LPA and 2025 Amendment No. 9, effective 7 December 2025):**

| Leave type | Entitlement | Pay | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Annual leave | 6 working days/year after 1 year of continuous service | Full pay | LPA Section 30. No statutory entitlement before 1 year. |
| Sick leave | Unlimited days as necessary | Employer pays max 30 days/year | Medical certificate required for 3+ consecutive days (LPA Section 32) |
| Maternity leave | 120 days per pregnancy (including holidays) | Employer pays up to 60 days' wages; SSO covers additional days | LPA Amendment 2025. Includes prenatal and postnatal. |
| Spousal/paternity leave | 15 days | Full pay (100%) | Must be taken within 90 days of birth (LPA Amendment 2025) |
| Childcare leave (newborn) | 15 consecutive days | 50% pay | For female employees when newborn has medical complications. Requires medical certificate. (LPA Amendment 2025) |
| Public holidays | Minimum 13 days/year | Full pay | As announced by government annually |
| Weekly holiday | Minimum 1 day/week | N/A | Typically Sunday; interval between days off max 6 days |
| Ordination leave | Up to 30 days (company policy) | Per company policy | For male Buddhist employees entering monkhood |
| Military service leave | Up to 60 days/year | Full pay | Per Thai law |
| Sterilization leave | As advised by doctor | Full pay | Per Thai law |

**Leave process:**
- Leave requests submitted to direct manager for approval before the leave period (except sick leave which can be notified on the day)
- Annual leave must be coordinated with team to ensure coverage
- Sick leave of 3+ days: medical certificate submitted upon return
- Maternity leave: advance notice to HR as early as possible to plan coverage
- All leave tracked and recorded by HR
- Unused annual leave: per company policy (Thai law does not require carry-over, but employer may offer it)

**Attendance:**
- Attendance tracked daily for all employees
- Late arrivals and early departures must be reported to direct manager
- Unexcused absences: 3 consecutive working days of abandonment constitutes grounds for termination without severance (per LPA Section 119)

**Tardiness escalation:**
- 1st–2nd late arrival in a month: verbal reminder from direct manager
- 3rd late arrival in a month: verbal warning documented by manager
- 4th+ late arrival in a month or pattern of consistent tardiness: written warning from HR
- Continued tardiness after written warning: enters PIP process (see Offboarding section)
- Tardiness records are tracked monthly and reviewed by HR
- "Late" is defined as arriving more than 15 minutes after scheduled start time

**PIP:**
- See Offboarding section above for full PIP process

**Security and external dealing rules:**
- Confidential company information must not be shared externally without authorization
- Creator personal data (tax ID, bank accounts) restricted to Finance and Legal
- Client commercial terms confidential — never shared across clients
- Platform partner confidential information never shared between competing platforms (see core-principles.md)
- Social media posts about ERIDU or clients require management approval
- Employees must not engage in business activities that compete with or conflict with ERIDU's interests

## Access management

### By role and level

| Level | Access scope |
|---|---|
| Individual contributor | Own pillar systems, shared workspace, relevant LINE groups |
| Team Lead | Pillar systems + team management tools, reporting dashboards |
| Head of Pillar | Full pillar access + cross-pillar shared resources, P&L data for own pillar |
| Country Manager | All systems, all pillars, all data except founder-restricted items |
| Founder | Full access |
| HR | HR systems, employee records, payroll data, access management |
| Finance | Finance systems, CRM financial data, creator tax profiles, bank details |

### Systems and folders

Access must be provisioned on onboarding and revoked immediately on offboarding:

- Google Workspace (email, Drive, Sheets, Docs) — by role and folder permissions
- Notion (knowledge base) — by pillar and role
- Northstar CRM — by schema (Sales, Product, Creator) and role
- LINE groups — by team and function
- Platform accounts (TikTok, Shopee, Lazada) — only for designated operators
- Studio equipment and physical access — only for Erify production staff

**Access review:** quarterly review of all system access to ensure accuracy (remove stale accounts, adjust for role changes)

## Recruiting process

### Full-time employees

1. **Org planning:** Founder and Country Manager decide on org structure and hiring needs
2. **Requirements:** Head of Pillar creates job requirements and submits to HR
3. **Sourcing:** HR sources candidates (job boards, referrals, outreach)
4. **Screening:** HR screens applications, shortlists candidates
5. **Interview:** HR schedules interviews with hiring manager (Head of Pillar or designated interviewer)
6. **Decision:** Hiring manager decides, subject to final Country Manager approval
7. **Offer:** HR prepares and extends offer
8. **Onboarding:** HR executes onboarding checklist (see above)

### Interns

1. HR sources, screens, interviews, and decides
2. Country Manager informed but approval not required
3. Internship agreement signed
4. Simplified onboarding checklist

### Contractors (MCs, freelancers)

- Pillar-specific: Erify Offset team manages MC recruitment (see content-management.md), ERISA manages creator recruitment (see creator-management.md)
- HR ensures: service contracts signed, all required documents collected and stored before onboarding, withholding tax compliance
- No SSO registration for contractors

## Payroll processing

### Monthly payroll timeline

| Date | Task | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| 1st–3rd | Collect attendance, leave, overtime data, and incentive calculations for previous month | HR |
| 3rd–5th | Calculate payroll: base salary, incentives, overtime, deductions (SSO, WHT, other) | HR |
| By 5th | Payroll prepared and submitted to Finance for payment | HR |
| 10th | Payroll payment released | Finance |

### Payroll components

- Base salary
- Incentives (performance-based, as applicable per role and pillar — calculated monthly)
- Overtime pay (1.5x working day, 2x holiday, 3x overtime on holiday — per LPA)
- Deductions: personal income tax (withholding), SSO contribution (5% of salary, max THB 875/month per party on ceiling of THB 17,500 effective Jan 2026)
- Employee Welfare Fund contribution: 0.25% for both employer and employee (effective October 2026, for employers with 10+ employees)

### Payroll records

- Payslips issued to all employees (digital acceptable)
- Payroll records retained minimum 2 years after termination (per LPA)
- Tax records retained minimum 5 years

### Quarterly

- Access management review (all systems)
- Org chart review and update

### Annually

| Deadline | Activity | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| January | Annual employment conditions report (Kor Ror 11) submitted to labor office | HR |
| February | Annual withholding tax summary (PND 1a) issued to all employees | External accountant (HR prepares) |
| February | Annual withholding tax certificates (50 Tawi) issued to all employees | HR / Finance |
| March | Employee personal income tax filing deadline (PND 90/91) — employees file their own | Employees (HR provides documents) |
| As needed | Work rules update and resubmission to labor office if changes made | HR |

### Ongoing compliance reminders

- New employee SSO registration: within 30 days of start date
- Work rules: must be in Thai, displayed at workplace, submitted to labor office (required when 10+ employees)
- Employee register: maintained in Thai, retained 2 years post-termination
- Minimum wage: check annually (Bangkok THB 372/day as of 2025, varies by province)
- Public holidays: confirm 13 government-announced holidays each year
