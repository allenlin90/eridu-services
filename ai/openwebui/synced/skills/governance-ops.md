# Governance Operations — ERIDU

## How we communicate

**Channel defaults:**
- LINE — quick communication, both internal and external (clients, creators, partners). Default channel for day-to-day coordination.
- Email — formal communications, confirmed records, contracts, financial documents, quotations, proposals. Anything that needs a paper trail.
- Calls — urgent and time-sensitive matters that cannot wait for a written response.

**Response time expectations:**
- Internal LINE: within 1 hour during business hours
- External LINE (clients, creators): within 1 hour during business hours
- Email: within 24 hours
- Calls: answer or return within the same business day

**Client communication:**
- Account owner is the primary point of contact for all client communication
- Team members may communicate directly with clients on operational matters (delivery coordination, scheduling, reporting)
- Any communication involving pricing, contract terms, escalation, or complaints must go through the account owner or Head of Pillar

**Creator communication:**
- Depends on the matter: Recruit team handles recruiting outreach, Creator Services team handles managed creator relationships, Campaign team handles campaign-related coordination
- Each team owns communication within their function — no cross-team creator communication without coordination

**Platform partner communication:**
- Head of Pillar, Country Manager, and designated partnership windows communicate with platform partners depending on the matter
- No one outside these roles communicates with platform partners without explicit approval
- Never make commitments to platform partners without Head-level or Country Manager approval

**Reporting standards:**
- Country Manager reports to Founder weekly + ad hoc for urgent matters
- Heads of Pillars report to Country Manager weekly (business review)
- Team Leads report to Heads within their pillar's daily standup and weekly team review
- All reports should be concise, decision-oriented, and flag blockers explicitly — no status updates without a "so what"

## How we document

**Principle:** If it's not documented, it didn't happen. Record-keeping is not optional — it protects the company, enables handoffs, and ensures continuity as the team scales.

**What must be documented:**
- Contracts and agreements — filed and accessible in the designated storage location
- Client communications and decisions — key decisions, approvals, and commitments recorded (not every message, but anything that creates an obligation or changes scope)
- Meeting outcomes and action items — see meeting format below
- Financial transactions — every payment, invoice, and expense must have supporting documents
- SOPs and processes — written down, not just verbal knowledge. If only one person knows how to do it, it's not documented.
- Changes to pricing, terms, or packages — any modification to standard terms must be recorded with the rationale and approver
- Creator contract changes — recorded in Northstar CRM (new contract row per renewal, never modify active contracts)

**Where documents live:**
- Knowledge base and SOPs: Notion
- CRM records: Northstar CRM
- Contracts, financial documents, formal records: Google Drive
- Each pillar is responsible for maintaining documentation within their domain
- Cross-cutting documents (finance, legal, HR) are maintained by the respective support function

**Ownership:** Every document must have a clear owner. The owner is responsible for keeping it current. Stale documentation is worse than no documentation — it creates false confidence.

## How we meet

**Meeting format:**
- Every meeting must have an agenda shared beforehand — no agenda, no meeting
- Meetings should have a fixed time limit. Respect the clock.
- Meeting notes must be recorded — decisions made, action items assigned, key discussion points captured
- Action items must have an owner and a deadline. No unassigned actions.
- Decisions made in meetings must be documented and shared with relevant stakeholders who were not present

**Meeting notes responsibility:** Each pillar decides their own approach for who takes notes and tracks action items. What matters is that it happens consistently, not who does it.

**Meeting records must include:**
- Date and attendees
- Agenda items covered
- Decisions made (with rationale if non-obvious)
- Action items with owner and deadline
- Items escalated or deferred

## How we review

**Daily — pillar standups:**
- Each pillar runs their own daily standup (Commerce, Erify, ERISA)
- Focus: yesterday's results, today's priorities, blockers
- Owned by: Head of Pillar or Team Lead

**Weekly — team reviews:**
- Within each pillar: Head of Pillar + Team Leads review operational performance
- Business weekly review: each Head of Pillar meets with Country Manager
- Focus: pipeline progress, delivery status, team capacity, issues requiring escalation
- Client account reviews: weekly check on active client accounts — delivery, GMV, satisfaction
- Creator portfolio reviews: weekly check on managed creator performance — GMV, activity, retention

**Monthly — performance and P&L:**
- Each Head of Pillar + Country Manager reviews pillar P&L and performance
- Country Manager reports monthly summary to Founder
- Focus: revenue vs. forecast, cost control, key wins, key risks, headcount
- Finance will join monthly reviews in a later phase

**Quarterly — business reviews (QBR):**
- Each pillar runs their own QBR
- Country Manager reviews with each pillar, then presents company-wide QBR to Founder
- Focus: quarterly results vs. targets, strategic progress, next quarter priorities, budget reforecast
- Platform partnership reviews: quarterly assessment of TikTok, Shopee, Lazada relationships — performance, opportunities, risks

**Annual — strategy and planning:**
- Company-wide strategic planning led by Founder + Country Manager
- Focus: annual targets, market positioning, pillar growth plans, investment priorities, organizational changes

## How we handle mistakes

**Principle:** Blameless, but not accountless. We focus on what happened and why — not who to punish. But we hold ourselves accountable for fixing the root cause and not repeating the same failure.

**When a post-mortem is required:**
- Any incident that affected a client relationship, platform partnership, or revenue
- Any breach of established SOPs or protocols
- Any financial loss or near-miss above the warning thresholds in financial-guardrails.md
- Any situation that triggered an "escalate immediately" action

**Post-mortem process:**
- Must be completed within 48 hours of the incident being resolved
- Must identify root cause, not just symptoms — ask "why" until you reach the process or system gap, not just the person
- Must produce concrete action items to prevent recurrence — each action item must have an owner and a deadline
- Must be shared with relevant stakeholders (Head of Pillar at minimum, Country Manager if the incident was escalated)
- Post-mortem records are kept as part of the team's documentation

**Repeated incidents:**
- If the same root cause produces a second incident, it triggers automatic escalation to Country Manager
- If a third incident occurs on the same root cause, it escalates to Founder
- Repeated incidents signal a systemic issue — the fix is process or structural change, not individual correction
