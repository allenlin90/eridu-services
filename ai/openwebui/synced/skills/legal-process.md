# Legal Process

---

## General

### What Legal covers

Legal is handled by the Country Manager with external counsel engaged as needed. There is no dedicated in-house legal team. Legal touches every pillar through contracts, compliance, data protection, and dispute resolution.

**Handled by:** Country Manager + external counsel (as needed)

**Key legal frameworks:**
- Thai Labor Protection Act B.E. 2541 and amendments (employment matters — see hr-ops.md)
- Thai Personal Data Protection Act B.E. 2562 (PDPA) — effective since June 2022, active enforcement from 2025
- Thai Revenue Code (tax compliance — see finance-ops.md)
- Platform partner Terms of Service (TikTok Shop, Shopee, Lazada)
- Thai Civil and Commercial Code (contracts)

### When to involve external counsel

- Contract disputes or litigation
- Regulatory inquiries or government investigations
- Complex contract negotiations (new partnership structures, non-standard terms)
- PDPA compliance gaps or data breach incidents
- Employment termination disputes
- Any matter where legal risk exceeds Country Manager's expertise

## Contract review

### Contract review workflow

1. **Initiation:** team member or Head of Pillar identifies need for contract (new client, vendor, creator, MC, employee, partnership)
2. **Draft:** use standard template where available. If no template exists or terms are non-standard, Country Manager reviews.
3. **Review:** Country Manager reviews all contracts before signing. External counsel engaged for complex or high-value contracts.
4. **Approval chain:**
   - Standard contracts (using approved templates, standard terms): Head of Pillar can approve
   - Non-standard terms or deviations: Country Manager approval required
   - High-value contracts (above 50,000 THB) or strategic partnerships: Founder approval required
   - Celebrity tier creator contracts: Founder approval required
5. **Signing:** only authorized signatories may sign on behalf of ERIDU (Country Manager or Founder)
6. **Filing:** signed contract stored in Google Drive with consistent naming. Contract details recorded in relevant CRM schema.

### Contract review SLA

- Standard contracts (template-based): reviewed within 3 business days
- Non-standard or complex contracts: reviewed within 7 business days
- Urgent contracts (platform deadlines, time-sensitive deals): same-day or next-day review — flag to Country Manager immediately

## Platform compliance

### Platform Terms of Service obligations

ERIDU's business depends on platform partnerships. Violating platform TOS is a "never happen" item (see core-principles.md).

**General obligations across all platforms:**
- Never violate platform content policies (prohibited products, misleading claims, fake reviews)
- Never manipulate platform algorithms or metrics (bot traffic, fake engagement)
- Never misuse platform data or share it with unauthorized parties
- Never misrepresent ERIDU's partnership status or capabilities
- Comply with all platform-specific creator management rules

**Platform-specific rules:**
- TikTok Shop: MCN partnership terms, creator management policies, content guidelines, affiliate program rules
- Shopee: SLP (Shopee Livestream Partner) program terms, affiliate program rules, shop management policies
- Lazada: partnership terms and content policies

**Platform neutrality (critical):**
- Never entice managed creators FROM TikTok TO Shopee (see creator-management.md)
- Never share confidential information between competing platforms
- Never publicly rank or compare platforms

**Monitoring:**
- Head of each Pillar responsible for staying current on platform TOS changes within their domain
- Platform TOS changes flagged to Country Manager
- Quarterly platform partnership review includes TOS compliance check

## Data privacy — PDPA compliance

### ERIDU's PDPA obligations

ERIDU is a data controller under the PDPA for personal data of creators, clients, employees, and contractors. PDPA compliance is incorporated into contracts but operational compliance across all functions needs strengthening.

### Personal data ERIDU collects and processes

| Data category | Data subjects | Sensitivity | Where stored |
|---|---|---|---|
| Contact info (name, email, phone, address) | Clients, vendors, creators, employees | General | Northstar CRM, Google Drive |
| National ID, tax ID | Creators, employees, contractors | Sensitive — restricted access | Northstar CRM (creator_tax_profiles), HR files |
| Bank account details | Creators, employees, contractors | Sensitive — restricted access | Northstar CRM (creator_bank_accounts), HR files |
| Health data (sick leave certificates) | Employees | Sensitive | HR files |
| Platform account data | Creators | General | Northstar CRM |
| GMV and performance data | Creators, clients | General (commercial) | Northstar CRM |

### PDPA compliance checklist

**Collection and consent:**
- [ ] Privacy notice provided to all data subjects before or at the time of data collection
- [ ] Consent obtained for collection of sensitive data (national ID, tax ID, bank details, health data)
- [ ] Purpose of data collection clearly stated and limited to stated purposes
- [ ] Consent is explicit, granular, and logged — not bundled into other agreements

**Processing and storage:**
- [ ] Data used only for stated purposes — no secondary use without additional consent
- [ ] Access restricted by role (Finance and Legal only for sensitive creator/employee data)
- [ ] Soft delete on all records — no hard deletion (aligns with both PDPA retention and CRM policy)
- [ ] Data retention periods defined and documented

**Data subject rights:**
- [ ] Process in place for data subjects to access, correct, delete, or port their data
- [ ] Requests responded to within 30 days
- [ ] Right to withdraw consent honored without penalty

**Security:**
- [ ] Appropriate technical and organizational security measures in place
- [ ] Access management reviewed quarterly (see hr-ops.md)
- [ ] Employee training on data handling practices

**Breach notification:**
- [ ] Breach response plan in place
- [ ] PDPC notified within 72 hours of a breach
- [ ] Affected data subjects notified without delay for serious breaches
- [ ] Breach register maintained

**Third-party processors:**
- [ ] Data processing agreements in place with all third parties handling personal data (e.g., external accountant, platform APIs)
- [ ] Third parties comply with PDPA security requirements

**PDPA compliance status:** Partially compliant — incorporated into most contracts, but operational compliance across all functions needs gap assessment. Recommended: conduct formal PDPA gap assessment with external counsel.

## Dispute resolution

### Process

1. **Internal assessment:** Country Manager assesses the dispute (client, creator, vendor, or employee)
2. **Direct negotiation:** attempt resolution through direct communication with the other party
3. **Mediation:** if direct negotiation fails, consider mediation (Labor Court mediation for employment disputes, commercial mediation for business disputes)
4. **External counsel:** engage external counsel if dispute escalates or involves significant legal risk
5. **Litigation:** last resort — Country Manager + Founder decision

### Escalation triggers

- Any legal threat received (letter, lawsuit, regulatory notice): escalate to Country Manager + Founder immediately
- Employment disputes: follow PIP and termination process in hr-ops.md before any termination
- Creator disputes: Head of Creator assesses first, escalates to Country Manager if unresolved
- Client disputes: Head of Commerce assesses first, escalates to Country Manager if unresolved
- Platform disputes: Country Manager handles directly — platform relationships are critical

### Documentation

- All disputes documented from first notice through resolution
- All communications with opposing parties retained
- Settlement agreements reviewed by Country Manager (external counsel for significant amounts)

## Document retention

| Document type | Minimum retention | Legal basis | Storage |
|---|---|---|---|
| Employment records | 2 years after termination | Thai Labor Protection Act | HR files (Google Drive) |
| Payroll and tax records | 5 years | Thai Revenue Code | Finance files (Google Drive) |
| Contracts (all types) | Duration of contract + 5 years | Statute of limitations | Google Drive (Legal folder) |
| Financial documents (invoices, receipts) | 5 years | Thai Revenue Code | Finance files (Google Drive) |
| PDPA consent records | Duration of processing + 5 years | PDPA | CRM + Google Drive |
| Breach notification records | 5 years | PDPA | Legal files |
| CRM data | Soft delete only — retained indefinitely | Company policy + PDPA | Northstar CRM |

## Key metrics, SLAs, and operational cadence

### SLAs

- Standard contract review: 3 business days
- Non-standard contract review: 7 business days
- Urgent contract review: same-day or next-day
- PDPA data subject requests: respond within 30 days
- Breach notification to PDPC: within 72 hours
- Dispute documentation: initiated within 24 hours of first notice

### Operational cadence

**As needed:**
- Contract review and approval
- Dispute resolution
- External counsel engagement

**Quarterly:**
- Platform TOS compliance review (part of platform partnership review)
- Contract template review (are templates still current?)
- PDPA compliance spot check

**Annually:**
- Full contract template review with external counsel
- PDPA gap assessment (recommended until full compliance achieved)
- Document retention audit (purge expired records per retention schedule)
- Legal budget review
