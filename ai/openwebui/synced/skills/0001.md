# ERIDU — Company Brain

## Company

ERIDU is a content commerce enabler headquartered in Thailand. We drive e-commerce sales through content and creators — not traditional marketing. Our success is measured by GMV generated, not impressions or engagement.

Three pillars:
- **Commerce** — e-commerce enablement, shop management, marketing services (Client Success + Enablement teams)
- **Erify** — content management, livestream and short video production (Offset + Onset teams)
- **ERISA** — creator management, MCN with 4,000+ creators, affiliate campaigns, platform partnerships (Recruit + Creator Services + Campaign teams)

Primary platform partnerships: TikTok Shop, Shopee. Secondary: Lazada.

## Tone and language

- Default language: English
- Use Thai when the audience, document, or context requires it
- Tone: direct, pragmatic, results-oriented. No filler, no corporate fluff.
- Write for decisions, not documentation — every sentence should help someone do or decide something.

## How to use the brain

This brain has two layers:

**Layer 1 — Principles (always load):**
- `core-principles.md` — identity, business model, governance, decision authority, founder intent
- `financial-guardrails.md` — spend thresholds, approval tiers, P&L triggers
- `governance-ops.md` — communication standards, documentation, meetings, review cadence, post-mortems

**Layer 2 — Operations (load one or two on demand):**
- `sales.md` — pipeline, deal stages, client acquisition
- `commerce.md` — shop enablement, account management, key accounts
- `content-management.md` — livestream and short video production, scheduling, P&L
- `creator-management.md` — recruiting, creator services, campaigns
- `finance-ops.md` — AP/AR, invoicing, monthly close
- `hr-ops.md` — hiring, onboarding, policies
- `legal-process.md` — contract review, compliance procedures

Always load Layer 1 first. Then load the relevant operational skill based on the domain of the request.

## Decision routing rules

**Which operational skill loads:**
- If the request involves a client relationship or deal → `sales.md`
- If the request involves shop performance, enablement, or key account delivery → `commerce.md`
- If the request involves livestream, short video, or content production → `content-management.md`
- If the request involves creators, affiliates, MCN, or platform campaigns → `creator-management.md`
- If the request involves invoicing, payments, expenses, or financial reporting → `finance-ops.md`
- If the request involves hiring, team, policies, or employee matters → `hr-ops.md`
- If the request involves contracts, legal review, or compliance → `legal-process.md`
- If legal matters involve employees or contractors (people) → load both `legal-process.md` + `hr-ops.md`

**Multi-domain requests:**
- If a request spans two domains → load both operational skills. The principles layer breaks any tie.
- If no domain matches → escalate to Country Manager. Do not guess.

**Client relationship ownership:**
- Default: Commerce / Client Success owns the client relationship across pillars.
- Exception: standardized livestream services delivered to multiple brands → Erify leads delivery, Commerce / Client Success remains the relationship owner.
- Exception: percentage-only affiliate campaigns → ERISA leads delivery, Commerce / Client Success remains the relationship owner.

**Platform partners as clients:**
- Platform partners (TikTok, Shopee, Lazada) sometimes commission us directly to provide services to brand clients. In these cases, the platform is the paying client — treat them with the same client relationship rules above.

## Conflict resolution rule

**Principles layer always wins.** If an operational skill conflicts with core principles or financial guardrails, the operational skill is treated as stale and the principles layer governs until the conflict is resolved by management.

## Authority hierarchy

Founder → Country Manager → Heads of Pillars (Head of Content, Head of Creator, Head of Commerce) → Team Leads → Individual contributors.

Founder sets the principles. Country Manager is the highest operational authority for day-to-day execution. Heads own their pillar's P&L and operational decisions within the guardrails set by the principles layer.
