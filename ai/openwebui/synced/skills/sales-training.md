# Sales Training — The Conversation Playbook

This skill covers **how to sell in the conversation** — scripts, tactics, and frameworks for reps.
For **process and policy** (pipeline stages, CRM, quotations, commission), see `sales.md`.
Pricing deviations and commercial commitments always follow `financial-guardrails.md`.

## How to use this skill (for the AI)

When a rep brings a situation:

1. **Objection quoted?** → Identify which of the 3 Leaks it is (Module 1), pull the matching Battle Card (Module 4), adapt the script to the specific client.
2. **Price pressure / counter-offer?** → Module 5 (Negotiation). Never draft a straight discount — always trade or slice scope. Flag that any deviation from rate card needs Head of Commerce or Country Manager approval per financial-guardrails.md.
3. **Deal stalled or ghosted?** → Run the MEDDIC check (Module 9) to find the missing letter, then pick a Ghost-Buster tactic (Module 8) or Value Drip touchpoint (Module 7).
4. **Preparing for a meeting?** → Modules 2–3: qualification, budget alignment (Two-Pocket), Data Wedge prep, opener, diagnosis buckets.
5. **Client wants "everything" or contact can't sign?** → Two-Pocket Protocol, Authority Steer, Wrong Window bridges (Module 2).

Always adapt scripts to the client's category, platform (TikTok Shop / Shopee), and numbers. Never send a template with placeholders unfilled.

## Module 1: Mindset & the 3 Leaks

**The core philosophy: "The Consultant"**

- **Role:** Strategic growth partner — not just a service provider.
- **The Waiter (junior rep):** takes orders, asks "What do you want?", pitches immediately.
- **The Consultant (senior rep):** diagnoses. Asks "Where does it hurt?" Prescribes only *after* understanding the pain.
- **The rule:** If you pitch before you diagnose, you lose.

**Why we lose deals — the 3 Leaks.** When a client says "no", identify which leak it is:

1. **Commodity Leak** — "Agency B is 30k, you are 50k." *Diagnosis:* you failed to differentiate; they think we're identical to the cheap option.
2. **Risk Leak** — "We aren't sure this will work." *Diagnosis:* they don't trust the outcome; you sold features instead of safety.
3. **Urgency Leak** — "Let's revisit next quarter." *Diagnosis:* the pain isn't sharp enough yet; you didn't use the Data Wedge to show the cost of waiting.

Each leak maps to a Battle Card category in Module 4.

## Module 2: The Conversion Filter

**Objective:** Don't reject "Tourists" (unprepared leads) — educate them into Buyers using **Anchor & Steer**.

### 1. The Vision Anchor (no target)

*Scenario:* You ask for a revenue target; they say "We aren't sure, we just want to grow."

> "I understand — many brands aren't sure what's realistic on TikTok yet. Let me share a benchmark: brands in your category usually aim for [X THB] in their first 90 days to make the ROI make sense. What's the upper and lower range of ROI for you? If we built a plan to hit that specific number, would that be a success for you?"

### 2. The Authority Steer (not the boss)

*Scenario:* You're talking to a marketing junior who can't sign.

> "This strategy looks solid. Since this involves a budget over [X], typically the GM or Director needs to approve the commercial terms. To save you from playing 'messenger' and answering tough questions alone, why don't we set up a 15-minute technical review with your Director next Tuesday? I'll handle the technical questions for you."

### 3. The Commitment Ladder (building momentum)

Don't ask for the marriage (contract) immediately. Get the client to invest small amounts of effort or emotion repeatedly — the more they participate, the harder it is to walk away (the IKEA effect).

**Rung 1 — Low-friction ask (during the call).** Goal: get them following your lead.
- *Screen share:* "Instead of me guessing, would you mind sharing your screen for 30 seconds? I want to see exactly how your current agency set up the voucher tab — it tells me if the error is technical or strategic."
- *Calculator:* "Do you have a calculator handy? Type in your current traffic… now multiply by a 2% conversion rate… what number do you see?" (When *they* calculate the number, they believe it.)

**Rung 2 — Tangible ask (post-meeting).** Goal: physical or digital investment.
- *Sample ask (content/live):* "I have an idea for a hook, but I need to feel the packaging weight on camera. Can you messenger one sample unit to my office tomorrow, before I write the proposal?"
- *Data ask (strategy):* "So I don't give you a cookie-cutter plan, could you export last month's Traffic Source CSV and email it to me? I want to customize to your peak hours."

**Rung 3 — Co-creation ask (pre-proposal).** Goal: psychological ownership. If they help write the plan, they won't reject it.
- *Draft review:* "I've sketched the scope. Before I formalize pricing, can I send a rough screenshot of the timeline? Tell me if these dates look realistic for your team."

**Rung 4 — Social ask (pre-close).** Goal: integrating into their tribe.
- *Introduction:* "This needs coordination with your design team. Okay if we start a quick LINE group with your designer, just to say hi? No work assigned yet." (Once you're in a LINE group with their staff, you're "hired" in their minds before signature.)

### 4. The Two-Pocket Protocol (budget alignment)

**The problem:** Clients say "We want to go viral AND get sales." These are two different pockets with two different strategies.

- **Pocket A (Branding/Visibility):** measured by views, reach, noise — macro influencers, high-production video.
- **Pocket B (Performance/Conversion):** measured by GMV, ROAS, orders — livestream, affiliates, hard-sell content.
- **The risk:** Sell a Pocket A product but get judged on Pocket B metrics → fired in month 3.

**The diagnosis question** (when they want "everything"):

> "To build the right deck, I need to know how you'll grade our homework. Gun to your head: if we post a campaign that gets 1 million views but zero sales, is that a success or a failure?"

- "Success" → Pocket A. Pitch branding/SOW packages.
- "Failure" → Pocket B. Pitch performance packages.
- "Both" → split the budget:

> "We can do both, but we shouldn't use the same dollar for two jobs. I recommend 70/30: 70% into the Performance Engine (livestream/shop ops), measured on GMV; 30% into the Visibility Engine (viral content/influencers), measured on views. Does that split fit your priorities, or should we adjust the ratio?"

**Re-alignment scripts:**
- *Performance client asking for "pretty" videos:* "You're measuring us on GMV. To hit that target we can't focus on TV-commercial style videos — we need hard-sell livestreams and quantity over quality. Are you okay with content looking more 'raw' if it means hitting the revenue target?"
- *Branding client asking for sales guarantees:* "You're measuring us on reach and image. With macro influencers the CPM is higher — we'll see a traffic lift, but we can't guarantee day-one ROAS like performance ads. Are you comfortable classifying this budget as 'marketing expense' rather than 'sales cost' internally?"

### 5. The Wrong Window (bridging departments)

**The problem:** You're pitching a launch/awareness campaign to a Performance Manager judged strictly on ROAS. They *literally cannot* sign it — it would ruin their KPI. You need the Brand Manager.

**Bridge (Performance → Brand):**

> "I agree — taking this from your performance bucket would destroy your ROAS because the CPM is too high. You shouldn't be paying for this. But this campaign drives the search volume you need for cheaper clicks later. Does your marketing team have a separate 'new launch' or 'awareness' budget? If they cover top-of-funnel, you focus your budget on converting it — that protects your ROAS. Should we set up a joint chat with the Brand Manager?"

**Reverse bridge (Brand → Performance):**

> "I hear you — livestream and affiliates feel too 'hard sell' for the premium image you've built. Usually the E-Commerce Director loves this channel because it clears inventory and generates cash flow. Why don't we frame it as a purely commercial channel, separate from your brand channels? Is there an E-Commerce or Sales Lead we should loop in? If they have a sales target, they may fund this engine so you don't have to dirty your hands with it."

## Module 3: The Sales Process (in-meeting)

*This is the conversation layer over the pipeline in sales.md (pre-pitch → pitch → proposal → close).*

**Step 1 — Preparation (the Data Wedge).** Before the meeting, use Kalodata / Shopee backend to research their competitors and category leaders. Build the slide: "Competitor X making 2M THB/month" vs "Client making 100K." Psychology: loss aversion — they're losing market share every month they wait.

**Step 2 — Meeting opener (frame control):**

> "Hi [Name]. We have 30 minutes. To make the best use of your time, I've prepared a quick audit of your shop performance vs your competitors. My goal is to see if there are gaps we can fill. If we can help, I'll show you how; if not, I'll point you in the right direction. Does that sound fair?"

**Step 3 — Diagnosis (the Gap).** Do not show the solution yet. Fill three buckets first:

1. **Current situation** — performance, quality, work process, team capacity, timeline.
2. **Expectation** — same dimensions, target state.
3. **The Pain (the math):** "Let me play this back. You're at 1M THB. You need 3M by December — that's a 2M gap. The million-baht question: what's the main bottleneck stopping you from closing that gap right now? Why haven't you hit it yet?"

**Step 4 — Presentation (the solution).**
- Transition: "Based on that, I recommend we skip the Basic Package and look at the Growth Module, because it specifically addresses [the bottleneck]."
- Deck flow: **Market Reality → The Gap → The Solution → Case Study.**

## Module 4: The Master Battle Cards (objection handling)

### Category 1 — PRICE ("too expensive") → Commodity Leak

**Scenario A: Commodity comparison** ("Agency B is 30k, you are 50k.")
*Pivot — cost vs risk:* "Strictly on price, they're cheaper. But in e-commerce the most expensive thing isn't the agency fee — it's wasted ad spend. Agency B charges less, but do they use [ERIDU differentiator] to deliver [outcome]? If we charge 20k more but sell 200k more inventory, which option is actually cheaper?"

**Scenario B: Budget ceiling** ("We only have 30k.")
*Pivot — reduce scope, not margin:* "We can respect that cap. To fit 30k we remove [scope item]. That gets you started immediately; we add the rest back next month once sales come in."

### Category 2 — IN-HOUSE ("we can do it ourselves") → Commodity Leak

**Scenario: Ego defense** ("My marketing team can do this.")
*Pivot — marketing vs ops:* "Your team is great at brand marketing. But TikTok Shop requires *operations*. Does your team have time for customer service chat at 9 PM? Do they know how to appeal a shop violation within 2 hours? We don't replace your team — we enable them so they can focus on strategy."

### Category 3 — TRUST & RISK ("will this work?") → Risk Leak

**Scenario: The guarantee** ("Can you guarantee 1M THB sales?")
*Pivot — inputs vs outputs:* "I can't ethically guarantee a sales number, because I don't control the market. But I guarantee the **inputs**: we will produce X videos and Y hours of livestream. If we miss our input targets, we work until we hit them."
> ⚠️ Guardrail: never commit an *output* (GMV) guarantee, and any input-guarantee or remedy clause beyond standard scope needs Head of Commerce approval before it goes in a proposal (financial-guardrails.md).

### Category 4 — URGENCY ("let's wait") → Urgency Leak

**Scenario: Kick the can** ("Let's revisit in Q1.")
*Pivot — the algorithm cost:* "We can wait. But shops with established history before [next month] get priority traffic. If you start in Q1, you start with a 'cold' account against competitors who built data now. I recommend a soft launch to warm the account."

## Module 5: Strategic Negotiation

**Core principle:** Price is arbitrary; value is concrete. Clients compare your **price** to a competitor's **price** — force them to compare **value to value**.

### Part 1 — Justify the rate (the "Apples to Oranges" script)

Use when they say "you're expensive":

> "The number looks higher on paper, but let's map cost to the correct value. Agencies charging 30k are usually task-based (posting videos). We are outcome-based (top-tier status + strategy + operations). If you hire the 30k agency but spend 10 hours a week managing them, your effective cost is 60k. With us the fee is higher but the management cost is zero. Mapped to actual net value, we're the lower-risk option."

### Part 2 — The "Trade, Don't Cave" rule

If price goes down, value must go down **visibly**. If you simply lower the price, you admit you were overcharging.

> ⚠️ Guardrail: any deviation from standard rate card — discount, scope trade, or payment-term concession — requires Head of Commerce or Country Manager approval before offering (financial-guardrails.md). Draft the trade, get approval, then present.

### Part 3 — Techniques

**A. Scope Slice (deduct value):**
> "I understand the budget is capped at 30,000 THB — we can hit that. To map our service to that price, we remove the [2 studio livestreams] and switch to home-stream setups. That lowers our cost structure while keeping the core strategy intact. Are you okay with that adjustment to the value?"

**B. Wince & Pause (reaction to lowballs):**
1. **Wince** — visibly react to show the number is painful.
2. **Silence** — 3 seconds.
> Client: "We can only pay 20k." Rep: "Oh…" *(long pause)* "That number… would require us to remove almost all the strategic support." *(pause again)*

**C. Cash Flow Trade (the banker):** trade price for liquidity.
> "We don't discount the retainer — margins are reinvested into the tools we run for your shop. But if you pay the full quarter upfront, I can justify waiving the setup fee to my finance team. You get the saving, we simplify cash flow. Does that work?"
(Aligns with payment-terms rules in financial-guardrails.md — upfront terms are always preferred.)

**D. Authority Lock (stop split-the-difference games):**
> "I want to make this work. I'm authorized to go as low as [X], not a penny lower — below that we lose money on the staff for this level of service. I can lock [X] in right now; otherwise I have to stick to the original proposal."
(Only quote a floor you actually have approval for.)

## Module 6: Advanced Closing Techniques

**A. Conditional Close (the if/then bridge):**
> "Hypothetically, if we were able to [fix the specific objection — e.g., adjust payment terms], would you be ready to sign the agreement by this Friday?"

**B. Calendar Back-Calculation (urgency):**
> "To have the shop warm for the 9.9 campaign we need 3 weeks lead time — the latest we can start onboarding is [date]. Are you comfortable missing the 9.9 window, or should we get the paperwork sorted today?"

**C. Scale-Up (conditional commitment):**
> "Let's not overcomplicate it. Let's proceed to the one-year agreement with a 90-day probation. If we hit [KPI] by day 90, we continue to the annual retainer; if we miss, we shake hands and part ways. Does that lower the risk enough to get started?"
Alternative: pitch a ColdStart engagement.

## Module 7: Strategic Nurturing (the Value Drip)

When a client says "wait," run this sequence (log each touchpoint in the CRM):

1. **Day 3 — Newsjack:** "Hi [Name], TikTok just updated the algorithm on livestream weights. This makes your content strategy even more urgent — here's a 2-sentence summary of what it means for you…"
2. **Day 7 — Competitor Alert:** "Hi [Name], keeping an eye on the market — [Competitor] just launched a bundle strategy on Shopee Live and sold [X units] in an hour. We could replicate this for you next week."
3. **Day 14 — Peer Success:** "Hi [Name], we just wrapped a campaign for [Client Y] who had the same bottleneck. Their traffic doubled in 10 days — attaching the 1-page breakdown."

## Module 8: The Ghost-Buster Protocol (re-engagement)

Escalate through these in order — Tactic D is the nuclear option, use last.

**A. Low-Pressure Nudge:**
> "Hi [Name], I imagine your inbox is a war zone right now. No need for a long update — just a quick 👍 if this project is still alive, or 👎 if you want me to pause things on my end."

**B. Asset Bait (give-to-get):**
> "Hi [Name], I was brainstorming for your brand and sketched a rough 30-second video script targeting the price objection we discussed. Want me to send it over? No strings attached."

**C. Internal Update (the face-save):**
> "Hi [Name], my Ops Director is asking for resource planning. I'm fighting to keep a slot open for your launch. Can I tell him to hold it for 48 more hours, or should I release it to the waitlist?"

**D. Negative Label (nuclear):**
> "Hi [Name], have you given up on the goal of hitting [revenue target] by Q4? If so, no hard feelings — I just want to close the file so I stop bothering you."

## Module 9: Deal Health Check (MEDDIC)

**Rule:** A stalled or ghosted deal usually means one of these 6 letters is missing. You cannot close until you have a clear answer for every letter. If you don't know — ask.

**M — Metrics.** What's the quantifiable economic gain? You can't close "awareness"; you close numbers.
> "If we're sitting here 6 months from now, what specific GMV number or ROI percentage do we need to hit for you to say 'this was the best investment we ever made'?"

**E — Economic Buyer.** Who has the P&L authority? A manager can only say "no."
> "When it comes to final commercial approval for a budget this size, does that sit with you, or with a Finance Director or the Owner?"
> If "the Owner": "Since they'll have ROI and contract questions, should we invite them to the last 15 minutes of our next call? I'll answer their financial questions directly so you don't have to."

**D — Decision Criteria.** What are they comparing us on?
> "Besides price, what are the top 3 things you're judging this decision on — speed of launch, creator quality, backend reporting? If you had to rank them, which is #1?"

**D — Decision Process.** How do they buy?
> "Say we present Friday and you love it — walk me through what happens next on your side. Three competitive quotes? Legal review? How long does that internal process usually take?"

**I — Identify Pain.** What happens if they do nothing?
> "To be direct: what happens if you don't fix this by Q4? Do you miss your yearly target? Does it hit inventory storage costs? How much is this actually costing you right now?"

**C — Champion.** Who sells for you when you're not in the room? A Champion has **power** and **interest** — not just niceness. **The test: give them homework.**
> "I know the CEO will ask why we're more expensive. To help you defend the budget, I'll send a 1-page ROI calculator slide — can you present it in your Monday meeting?"
- "Yes, send it" → you have a Champion. "I don't want to bother him" → you have no internal support.

## Guardrails (Layer 1 always wins)

- **Pricing:** rate-card deviations need Head of Commerce or Country Manager approval (financial-guardrails.md) — get approval *before* offering any discount, scope trade, or floor price.
- **Guarantees:** never guarantee GMV outputs. Input commitments beyond standard scope need approval before entering a proposal.
- **Focus on GMV outcomes in every pitch** — ERIDU is a content commerce enabler, not a marketing agency (core-principles.md).
- **Log everything:** all touchpoints, offers, and nurture activity in Northstar CRM per sales.md.
- **Platform neutrality:** never share one platform's confidential information with another (core-principles.md).
- **Commission** is governed by sales.md / financial-guardrails.md — nothing in this playbook changes payout rules.
