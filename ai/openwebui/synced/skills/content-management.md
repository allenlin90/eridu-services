# Content Management — Erify

---

## General

### What Erify does

Erify is ERIDU's content production pillar — the studio and production engine that delivers livestreams and short videos driving commerce on TikTok Shop and Shopee. Erify operates 14+ studio livestream bases with 50,000+ hours of production experience.

**Teams:**
- Offset team — pre-production (planning, scheduling, content strategy, preparation, MC management)
- Onset team — live production (running livestreams, studio operations)

**Owned by:** Head of Content

**Client relationship:** Commerce / Client Success owns the client relationship. Erify delivers production. For standardized livestream services delivered to multiple brands (e.g., Shopee SLP), Erify leads delivery but Commerce remains the relationship owner.

### Revenue models

- Flat fees for livestream services (standard and premium tiers)
- % on GMV from livestream performance
- Short video production fees (currently outsourced to ERISA)
- Combination (flat fee + % on GMV) depending on client agreement

### Cost structure

**Variable costs (~400K THB/month):**
- MC cost per hour (primary variable cost driver — target <250 THB/hour for standard shows)
- Moderator cost per hour (premium shows only)

**Fixed costs (~400K THB/month):**
- Operator cost (operators cover all shows by shifts)
- Payroll and studio rent

**Margin drivers:** MC utilization + studio occupancy, number of livestream hours sold, premium vs standard mix, GMV performance (% take), MC cost per hour.

### P&L ownership

Erify owns its own P&L — it is a profit center, not a cost center. P&L is reviewed monthly by show type (standard vs premium) and by account.

## Livestream products

Erify delivers two main livestream types. Everything else is project-based.

### Standard livestream

- Main deliverable: delivery of SOW (scope of work)
- Must meet QC SLA requirements (see Production Operations)
- MC cost must be <250 THB/hour
- Can use any studio size (S, M, L) or virtual studio
- iPhone streaming is the default
- At least 1 highlight or full clip per brand per month saved to media library (Bee Station)

### Premium livestream (SpatialStream)

- Main deliverable: GMV performance
- Must meet QC requirements
- Only M and L studios (real backdrops required)
- Must have in-studio moderator assigned for each show
- Must ensure LoS (Look of Success) matches internal SpatialStream standard
- Must ensure delivery of SPCC mechanics (Sales mechanics, Product mechanics, Content mechanics, Creator mechanics)
- Must use a computer with OBS for streaming
- At least 1 highlight or full clip per brand per month saved to media library (Bee Station)

### Project-based

- Custom livestream engagements that don't fit standard or premium definitions
- Scoped and priced on a case-by-case basis

### Operating hours and peak times

- Studio is technically open 24 hours (security guard on site overnight)
- Typical livestream hours: 8am–10pm
- Campaign days: can extend until 2am the next day
- Peak hours: 12pm–2pm and 8pm–10pm (highest MC and studio demand)
- Campaign days: typically double-digit dates (e.g., 10.10, 11.11), mid-month, and payday campaigns
- Campaign days account for ~30% of total livestreams per month

## Production operations

### Shift structure

- Operators cover all shows by shifts
- 1 duty manager (owner) assigned to each shift
- Duty manager is responsible for everything that happens during their shift

### Pre-show checklist

Before every livestream, the production team must ensure:
- Livestream account login tested and working
- Samples displayed and properly managed
- Equipment functioning and fully charged
- MC follows the LoS (Look of Success) and QC SLA requirements
- Product carts set up correctly

### QC SLA

| SLA item | Target |
|---|---|
| Missing shows | 0% |
| Late shows | 0% |
| Violations (platform or brand policy) | 0% |
| LoS approval rate | 100% |
| Ticket fulfillment rate | 100% |
| Product carts set up correctly | 100% |

### Daily SCRUM

- Preparation and status of each show reviewed daily in SCRUM
- Focus: today's show lineup, readiness status, blockers, yesterday's issues

## Scheduling

### Process

**Monthly scheduling (by 25th of each month):**
- Full schedule for the following month must be finalized and locked by the 25th of the current month
- Brand brief and LoS must be approved and confirmed by account before scheduling is finalized

**Daily show status adjustments:**
- Any adjustments to the daily show status (changes, additions, cancellations) must be made and locked by 11am the following day
- No adjustments after this window without Head of Content approval

**Weekly schedule review and lock:**
- Schedules are reviewed and confirmed weekly
- Weekly review is approved by Country Manager
- Once approved, no changes may be made to the schedule of the next 7 days
- Any exception to the 7-day lock requires explicit Country Manager approval

### Key constraints (must be strictly followed)

| Constraint | Target | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Average monthly MC cost | <250 THB/hour | Controls variable cost per standard show |
| Dual-MC slots | <1% of total shows | Dual-MC happens from scheduling inefficiency — minimize |
| Campaign MC usage | <5% of total shows | Campaign MCs cost up to 500 THB/hour — expensive overflow |

### Scheduling logic

- Standard MCs must cover at least 2 peak sessions (12pm–2pm, 8pm–10pm) before any additional sessions can be scheduled
- Flexible MCs cover peak and campaign sessions when Standard MCs can't cover
- Campaign MCs are last resort — only when Standard and Flexible can't cover demand
- Virtual studios used as overflow when physical studios are fully booked

## MC management

MC management is owned by the Offset team.

### MC types

All MCs are contractors, not employees.

| Type | Coverage | Cost | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|
| Standard MC | Must cover at least 2 peak sessions per day (12pm–2pm, 8pm–10pm). Additional sessions can be added but cannot be scheduled without peak coverage first. | Target <250 THB/hour | Primary workforce |
| Flexible MC | Covers peak and campaign sessions when Standard MCs aren't sufficient | Varies | Overflow for peaks/campaigns |
| Campaign MC | Used when both Standard and Flexible can't cover demand | Up to 500 THB/hour | Last resort — must stay <5% usage |

### MC onboarding

- All MCs are contractors — contracts and documents must be fully collected and stored before onboarding
- New MC recruitment is based on monthly P&L requirement (don't recruit ahead of demand)

### MC performance

- MCs must fulfill requirements of key mechanic delivery and LoS
- MC performance reviewed at least weekly
- MC cost reviewed weekly

## P&L planning and review

### Monthly P&L review

**Attendees:** Head of Content + Country Manager

**Review structure — by show type (standard vs premium) and by account:**

1. **GMV performance review**
   - GMV by show type and by account
   - GMV trends and deviations from plan

2. **Variable cost review**
   - Average MC cost per hour (target <250 THB for standard)
   - Average moderator cost per hour (premium shows)
   - MC cost trends and efficiency

3. **Fixed cost review**
   - Operator cost (shift coverage)
   - Fixed overhead (~400K: payroll + studio rent)

4. **Resource allocation planning**
   - MC allocation: how many Standard, Flexible, Campaign MCs needed next month
   - Studio allocation: which studios for which show types
   - Operator and moderator allocation
   - New MC recruitment needs based on P&L

## Key metrics, SLAs, and operational cadence

### Key metrics

**Production:**
- Missing show rate (target: 0%)
- Late show rate (target: 0%)
- Violation rate — platform or brand policy (target: 0%)
- LoS approval rate (target: 100%)
- Ticket fulfillment rate (target: 100%)

**MC:**
- Average MC cost per hour (target: <250 THB for standard)
- Dual-MC slot rate (target: <1%)
- Campaign MC usage rate (target: <5%)
- MC utilization rate

**Studio:**
- Studio occupancy rate
- Physical vs virtual studio split

**Financial:**
- Revenue by show type (standard vs premium) and by account
- GMV from premium shows
- Pillar P&L (margin by show type)

### SLAs

- Missing shows: 0%
- Late shows: 0%
- Violations (platform or brand policy): 0%
- LoS approval rate: 100%
- Ticket fulfillment rate: 100%
- Product carts set up correctly for every show
- Brand brief and LoS confirmed before scheduling
- MC contracts and documents collected before onboarding

### Operational cadence

**Daily:**
- SCRUM: show lineup, readiness, blockers, yesterday's issues
- Onset team manages live shows per shift with duty manager

**Weekly:**
- Scheduling review: schedule confirmed and approved by Country Manager; no changes to next 7 days after approval
- MC performance review
- MC cost review
- Team review: Head of Content + Team Leads

**Monthly:**
- P&L review by show type and account: Head of Content + Country Manager
- Resource allocation planning (MC, studio, operator, moderator)
- New MC recruitment decisions based on P&L
- Pillar P&L review reported to Country Manager

**Quarterly:**
- QBR: Erify pillar → Country Manager → Founder
- Studio capacity and equipment review
- Premium vs standard mix optimization

**Annually:**
- Studio expansion or consolidation planning
- MC workforce strategy
- New product development (e.g., bringing short video in-house)
