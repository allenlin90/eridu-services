# biggieG — ERIDU Talent Development Framework
**Quarterly Planning, Development & Review of Team Members**

**Audience:** Managers (Team Leads, Heads of Pillar, Country Manager) and HR
**Cadence:** Quarterly cycle, synchronized with QRVs (quarterly reviews)
**Version:** 1.1 — July 2026 | **Owner:** Country Manager + HR | **Review:** Quarterly

> **Terminology (v1.1):** QSG = company/pillar-level Quarterly Strategic Goal, set at the company QRV and recorded in `quarterly-strategic-goals.md`. Each QSG is broken down into multiple initiatives — smaller weekly/monthly goals — and team members are assigned to initiatives, not to QSGs directly. What v1.0 of this document called a member's "QSG" is now an initiative.

> **Related documents:**
> - `quarterly-strategic-goals.md` — the company/pillar QSGs and key metric targets for the quarter; initiatives should ladder into these
> - `eridu-operational-metrics-framework.md` — the company metrics that all initiative outputs must ladder up to
> - `hr-confidential.md` — salary bands, incentive rates, bonus pool, promotion salary adjustments (**restricted access: Founder, CM, HR only — never quote its contents through this framework**)
> - `hr-ops.md` — general compensation and promotion policy

## 1. Purpose

biggieG is ERIDU's operating system for talent development. It gives every manager one consistent, repeatable loop to grow their people, and gives HR and management a common language to evaluate readiness, promotion, and compensation across pillars.

The framework has three linked mechanisms:

1. **Assess** — map each team member's current skill level across three areas: **Universal Skills**, **Domain Skills**, and **Governance Areas**, against the L0–L3 competency matrix (Section 4).
2. **Develop via initiatives** — assign or adjust initiatives: tasks, duties, or responsibilities deliberately chosen to exercise the skills the member currently lacks for their next level. Initiatives are the building blocks of the company/pillar QSGs: each QSG breaks down into multiple weekly/monthly initiatives, and members are assigned at the initiative level. Every initiative output must align with and answer to a company-tracked metric, and ladder into a pillar/company QSG where applicable (Section 6).
3. **Review & Reward** — promotion and compensation are evaluated and adjusted based on demonstrated progress on the matrix and delivery of initiative outcomes (Sections 7–8).

The core principle: **we do not develop people in the abstract.** Skill growth is engineered through real work that moves company metrics. If it develops a skill but produces no metric-relevant output, it is not a development initiative. If a task moves a metric but stretches no skill gap, it is business-as-usual, not development.

## 2. The Level Framework

| Level | Label | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **L0** | Probation | New hire in probation (119-day contract / 90-day internal). Learning tools, SOPs, and culture. Delivering assigned tasks error-free. |
| **L1** | Function | Confirmed individual contributor. Owns a functional module end-to-end; solves issues independently; primary PoC for routine partner requests. |
| **L2** | Team Lead | Owns team output quality and members' professional growth. Manages team bandwidth, budget, and cross-departmental influence. |
| **L3** | Manager | Owns departmental P&L, strategy, organizational design, and talent uplift velocity. Shapes culture and market positioning. |

**Rules of the level system:**
- A member's level is a single, whole-person designation — but their skill map is granular: they may sit at L2 in some skills and L1 in others. The skill map (not the title) drives initiative design.
- **Promotion requires the full row**: a member is promotable to level N only when they demonstrate level-N behavior across **all skill categories** for a sustained period (see Section 7 — 3+ months of evidence, per promotion policy).
- L0 → L1 transition is governed by probation confirmation, not this promotion process — but the L0 column defines the bar a probationer must clear.

## 3. The Quarterly Cycle

Synchronized with the company review cadence (Metrics Framework §7):

| Phase | When | What happens | Who |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Skill Assessment** | Last 2 weeks of quarter | Member submits the Self-Assessment Form (§5.1); manager scores against the matrix; the Quarterly 1-on-1 (§5.3) reconciles both and identifies gaps to next level. | Manager + member; HR calibrates |
| **2. Initiative Assignment** | First 2 weeks of new quarter (alongside Pillar QRV, after the quarter's QSGs are set at the company QRV) | Manager drafts 2–4 initiatives per member targeting identified skill gaps, each mapped to a company metric and laddering into a pillar/company QSG where applicable. Member reviews and commits. | Manager drafts; Head of Pillar approves; CM approves Head-level initiatives |
| **3. Mid-Quarter Check** | Week 6–7 | Progress review on each initiative. Adjust scope if business priorities shifted (adjustment requires same approval as setting). | Manager + member |
| **4. Initiative Assessment** | Within 2 weeks after quarter end (aligned with bonus assessment window) | Score each initiative outcome; update skill map with new evidence; feed results into bonus proposal and promotion pipeline. | Manager proposes; Head of Pillar → CM approval chain |
| **5. Reward & Adjust** | Within 1 month of assessment | Bonus paid per policy; promotion cases submitted; next quarter's assessment baseline updated. | HR executes; approvals per hr-confidential |

**HR's role throughout:** calibration (is the same bar applied across pillars?), documentation (all assessments and initiative records on file), and pipeline tracking (who is approaching promotion readiness, who is stalled).

## 5. Step 1 — Skill Assessment

**How to score.** For each of the 10 skill categories, assign the member the **highest level whose behaviors they demonstrate consistently** (as a pattern over the quarter, not a one-off). Evidence must be concrete: work artifacts, metric results, ticket records, meeting outcomes, peer/partner feedback.

**Rating scale per skill category:**

| Rating | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Solid at level N** | Demonstrates all level-N behaviors consistently; ready to stretch toward N+1 |
| **Developing at level N** | Demonstrates most level-N behaviors; gaps noted |
| **Below level N** | Titled at N but not consistently demonstrating the row — triggers a development-priority initiative (or PIP if a performance issue, per hr-ops) |

**Process:**
1. Member completes the **Quarterly Self-Assessment Form** (Section 5.1) plus a self-rating against the matrix (same scale).
2. Manager completes independent assessment with written evidence per category, and prepares for the 1-on-1 by reading the form through the matrix (Section 5.2).
3. Manager and member hold the **Quarterly 1-on-1** (Section 5.3), reconcile differences, and agree on the gap map: which categories block the next level.
4. HR reviews assessments across pillars for calibration — the same behavior must earn the same rating in every team.
5. Output: an updated **Skill Map** (10 ratings + evidence notes) stored on the member's file. This is the input to initiative design.

**Anti-patterns to avoid:**
- Rating on effort or attitude instead of demonstrated behavior from the matrix rows.
- Averaging: a member "between L1 and L2" is L1 in that category until L2 behavior is consistent.
- Recency bias: assess the full quarter, not the last two weeks.

### 5.1 Quarterly Self-Assessment Form (filled by the employee)

Distributed by HR in the last 2 weeks of the quarter; must be submitted **at least 3 working days before the 1-on-1** so the manager has time to prepare. Answers should cite concrete examples wherever possible — the manager will map them to matrix behaviors and metrics.

**A. Job Responsibilities and Expectations**
1. What's your understanding of the expectations and goals of your role? How is this different from last quarter?
2. What's your understanding of your primary job responsibilities? How is it different from last quarter?
3. What's your understanding of your supporting duties? How is it different from last quarter?
4. How well have you met the expectations set for your role this quarter? Provide examples.
5. Are there any areas of your job that you feel are unclear or need clarification?
6. What are you supposed to do but have not done yet? And why?

**B. Performance Review**
1. What are your 3 biggest accomplishments during this quarter? And how were you able to achieve them?
2. What support did you get to help achieve these goals?
3. How does this contribute to meeting the team goals for this quarter?
4. What were your 3 biggest challenges during this quarter that you haven't overcome yet, and why?
5. What support do you need to help overcome these challenges?
6. How does this affect the team meeting its goals for this quarter?
7. What have you learned/developed this quarter that will help improve performance next quarter?

**C. Goals**
1. What are your goals for the next quarter?
2. What training, development opportunities, or support would help you achieve these goals?
3. How confident are you to achieve these goals? And why?
4. What could your manager do to better support your work?

**D. Working Relationships** *(repeat this block for up to 3 key day-to-day collaborators)*
1. Who do you work with in your day-to-day work?
2. How do they help you with your work?
3. What challenges do you have when working with him/her?
4. What can you do to improve this?
5. What can they do to improve this?
6. What can you do to help them improve this?

**E. Team & Other Feedback**
1. What are the 3 biggest accomplishments of the team during this quarter?
2. What were the 3 biggest challenges of the team during this quarter that were not overcome yet? And why?
3. Any other feedback.

### 5.2 How the manager reads the form (before the 1-on-1)

The form is raw material; the manager's preparation job is to translate answers into biggieG's three currencies — **matrix evidence, metric impact, and initiative input** — before the meeting. Each form section feeds a specific part of the loop:

| Form section | What it feeds in biggieG | What the manager extracts |
|---|---|---|
| A. Responsibilities & Expectations | Role clarity check + Accountability rating | Does the member's understanding match the manager's? Any drift signals a delegation/communication gap — possibly the *manager's* L2 Communication gap, not the member's. A6 ("supposed to do but not done") is direct Accountability evidence. |
| B. Performance Review | Skill Map evidence + initiative scoring | Map each accomplishment to a matrix category and level ("solved escalations independently" = Influence/Leadership L1–L2) and to the metric it moved. Map each unresolved challenge to the category it exposes — challenges are next quarter's initiative raw material. |
| C. Goals | Initiative drafting (Step 2) | Compare the member's stated goals against the manager's draft initiatives and the pillar's metric targets. C3 (confidence) calibrates initiative sizing; C2 and C4 become the "Support needed" field of each initiative. |
| D. Working Relationships | Universal Skills evidence (Communication, Influence/Leadership) + cross-team friction signals | Self-aware answers to D4–D6 are Mindset & Culture evidence. Recurring friction named by multiple team members around the same interface is an operational finding — escalate per pillar review, don't bury it in one person's file. |
| E. Team & Other Feedback | Calibration + team-level initiative themes | Compare against the team's actual metric results. A member who can accurately name the team's real accomplishments and blockers is demonstrating Critical Thinking at L2 trajectory. |

**Preparation checklist (30–45 min per member):**
1. Read the full form once without judging.
2. Annotate: tag each B-section item with `[category @ level]` and `[metric]`.
3. Complete your independent matrix rating **before** reading their self-rating — then compare.
4. List discrepancies to reconcile (rating gaps, expectation drift from Section A, support asks you can/can't grant).
5. Pre-draft 2–4 candidate initiatives so the meeting shapes them rather than starts from zero.

### 5.3 The Quarterly 1-on-1 — GEARS framework

One structured session per member per quarter (60–90 min; 45 min for L0), held during the assessment window. It follows the same logic as the framework itself: ground in facts, map to the matrix, anchor to metrics, then commit. Five stages — **GEARS**:

| Stage | Time | What happens | Form input |
|---|---|---|---|
| **G — Ground the role** | ~10% | Confirm shared understanding of expectations, primary responsibilities, and supporting duties. Resolve anything the member flagged as unclear. If understanding has drifted, fix the delegation, not just the member. | Section A |
| **E — Evidence review** | ~30% | Walk through accomplishments and undone items one by one. For each: *which matrix behavior does this demonstrate, at what level, and which metric did it move?* Use matrix language explicitly ("this is L2 Critical Thinking — root-cause analysis"). Praise and critique both attach to specific rows, never to personality. | Sections B1–B3, A4, A6 |
| **A — Align the Skill Map** | ~20% | Compare self-rating vs manager rating category by category. Where they differ, resolve with evidence — whoever has the concrete example wins. Output: the agreed 10-category Skill Map and the 1–2 categories that most block the next level. | Self-rating + manager rating |
| **R — Remove blockers & plan support** | ~20% | Review unresolved challenges and support asks. Classify each blocker: skill gap (→ initiative), resource/access gap (→ manager action with a date), or cross-team friction (→ escalate or coach per Section D findings). Manager commits explicitly to what they will provide — this becomes the "Support needed" field in the initiatives. | Sections B4–B6, C2, C4, D |
| **S — Set the initiatives** | ~20% | Co-shape next quarter's 2–4 initiatives from the manager's pre-draft and the member's stated goals. Verify each passes the three tests (skill, metric, measurability). Confirm checkpoints. Member leaves knowing exactly what "Exceeded / Achieved" looks like and how it connects to bonus. | Section C + manager pre-draft |

**Conversation principles (aligned with the core biggieG principles):**
- **Evidence over impression.** Every rating claim — theirs or yours — must attach to a concrete example. "I feel you're not ready" is not a valid sentence in this meeting; "the matrix says L2 requires X, and this quarter we saw Y" is.
- **Matrix as the shared language.** Feedback always names the category and the level behavior. This depersonalizes hard conversations and makes the path to promotion legible: the member should leave able to state which rows they must close.
- **Metrics as the anchor.** Development talk that never touches a company metric has drifted — bring it back to what the work must move.
- **Two-way, but bounded.** C4 and Section D feedback about the manager is heard, acknowledged, and where valid, converted into a manager commitment with a date. Compensation figures are never discussed in the 1-on-1 — bonus and salary follow the approval chain (Sections 7–8); the manager may explain the *mechanism*, never propose numbers.
- **No surprises.** Nothing in the quarterly 1-on-1 should be new information if mid-quarter checkpoints happened. If it is, that's a checkpoint failure to fix.
- **Document within 48 hours.** Manager files the agreed Skill Map, blocker classifications, manager commitments, and draft initiatives to HR. Undocumented outcomes don't exist for calibration, bonus, or promotion purposes.

**After the session:** draft initiatives go into the approval chain (Head of Pillar → CM where required); HR runs cross-pillar calibration on the Skill Maps; manager commitments get tracked like any other deliverable and are reviewed at the mid-quarter checkpoint.

## 6. Step 2 — Initiative Design

An initiative is a task, duty, or responsibility assigned to a member — the unit that breaks a company/pillar QSG (see `quarterly-strategic-goals.md`) down into weekly/monthly deliverables. Every initiative must satisfy **all three tests**:

1. **Skill test** — it exercises a specific matrix gap identified in Step 1 (name the category and the target-level behavior it develops).
2. **Metric test** — its output aligns with and answers to a metric the company tracks in the Operational Metrics Framework (name the metric).
3. **Measurability test** — success is defined upfront in observable terms (what is delivered, by when, at what quality bar). No subjective or discretionary goals — consistent with the incentive guidelines in HR policy.

**Initiative structure (per initiative):**

| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| Goal statement | What the member will deliver this quarter |
| Parent QSG | The pillar/company QSG this initiative ladders into (ID from `quarterly-strategic-goals.md`), where applicable |
| Skill gap targeted | Category + the specific next-level behavior from the matrix |
| Company metric served | The exact metric from the Metrics Framework this output answers to |
| Success criteria | Measurable definition of done (numbers, dates, quality bars) |
| Support needed | Coaching, access, budget, or tooling the manager commits to provide |
| Checkpoint | Mid-quarter checkpoint definition |

**Rules:**
- 2–4 initiatives per member per quarter. Fewer, bigger goals beat many small ones.
- At least one initiative should target the member's **weakest category blocking promotion**.
- Initiatives are defined at the start of the quarter by the manager (consistent with bonus policy: "each employee's quarterly goals defined at the start of the quarter by their manager" — a member's quarterly goals are their assigned initiatives).
- Approval chain: Manager drafts → Head of Pillar approves → CM approves Head-level initiatives.
- Mid-quarter adjustment allowed when business priorities shift, with the same approval chain. Document the change.

### 6.1 Metric alignment map — where initiative outputs must land

Every initiative's "company metric served" must come from the Metrics Framework. Use this map to find the right anchor by pillar:

| Member's pillar/function | Initiative outputs should answer to |
|---|---|
| **Erify (EF)** | Production SLA fulfillment (missing/late shows, violations, LoS approval, ticket fulfillment), scheduling efficiency (dual-MC rate <1%, campaign-MC rate <5%), total livestreams (standard/premium), GMV by show type & account |
| **ERISA (EA)** | Managed-creator GMV by tier, new creator GMV & count, churn GMV & count, superstar/non-superstar activeness, paid-package on-time delivery (45d standard / 30d express), ADP campaign GMV & active campaign count |
| **Commerce** | GMV fulfillment rate per account, ticket fulfillment, sales target achievement rate, sales outreach count, new vendor onboarding rate |
| **Finance** | Collection rate, AR aging, AP accuracy, reconciliation completion, monthly close timeliness, petty cash accuracy, filing on-time rate, cash runway reporting, DSO/DPO/CCC, payroll accuracy |
| **HR** | Payroll on-time rate, filing accuracy, recruiting target attainment, time to fill, probation completion rate, PIP resolution rate, attendance tracking, contractor document compliance |
| **Any pillar (stretch/leadership initiatives)** | North Star metrics: total GMV, revenue by pillar, gross/net margin, revenue concentration risk, cash conversion cycle |

### 6.2 Worked examples

**Example A — EA Creator Services, L1 targeting L2**
- *Gap:* Influence/Leadership at L2 ("drives consensus, coaches juniors, resolves external escalations").
- *Initiative:* Own the paid-package delivery pipeline for one client segment this quarter: coordinate 2 junior teammates, resolve creator-side escalations directly, and run the weekly delivery sync.
- *Metric served:* Paid-package on-time delivery rate (45-day standard SLA).
- *Success criteria:* ≥95% on-time delivery for the segment; zero escalations reaching the Head of Creator unresolved; documented weekly sync notes.

**Example B — EF Ops, L1 targeting L2**
- *Gap:* Critical Thinking at L2 ("root-cause analysis of recurring team blockers").
- *Initiative:* Diagnose why dual-MC slots occur, implement a scheduling fix, and report monthly.
- *Metric served:* Scheduling efficiency — dual-MC slot rate target <1%.
- *Success criteria:* Root-cause report delivered by week 4; dual-MC rate below 1% for the final two months of the quarter.

**Example C — Commerce Sales, L2 targeting L3**
- *Gap:* Accountability + Resource at L3 ("owns P&L results," "interprets P&L to plan budgets").
- *Initiative:* Build and own the quarterly revenue forecast for the SA client tier, including reforecast at mid-quarter, presented at the Pillar QRV.
- *Metric served:* Sales target achievement rate; revenue by pillar (Commerce).
- *Success criteria:* Forecast accuracy within an agreed variance band; reforecast delivered on time; CM sign-off on methodology.

**Example D — L0 probationer**
- *Gap:* Full L0 row (probation bar).
- *Initiative:* Complete all onboarding modules, pass competency tests on core tools, and deliver assigned daily tasks with zero missed deadlines and zero basic errors.
- *Metric served:* Probation completion rate (HR); plus the team's operational metric their daily tasks feed.
- *Success criteria:* Probation checklist 100% complete; supervisor attestation of error-free delivery; on track for confirmation at day 90/119.

## 7. Step 3a — Promotion

Promotion follows the confidential promotion framework (hr-confidential); biggieG operationalizes it through the matrix:

**Readiness definition.** A member is promotion-ready when their Skill Map shows **"Solid" at the next level in all 10 categories**, sustained across **at least 3 months** (one full assessed quarter, typically evidenced across two consecutive quarters), with the evidence coming primarily from delivered initiatives.

**Mapping matrix evidence to the promotion criteria:**

| Promotion criterion (per policy) | Where biggieG supplies the evidence |
|---|---|
| Technical/functional skills | Domain Skills ratings + initiative deliverables at next-level scope for 3+ months |
| Ownership | Accountability ratings + self-initiated initiative scope beyond current role |
| Decision-making | Critical Thinking ratings + manager attestation with initiative examples |
| Leadership (if applicable) | Influence/Leadership ratings + peer/subordinate feedback from team initiatives |
| Values alignment | Mindset & Culture ratings + no unresolved conduct issues |

**Process** (per confidential policy): Head of Pillar documents the case from the Skill Map and initiative record → HR reviews cross-pillar consistency → CM approves (Founder approves Head-level) → salary adjusted to the new level's band → effective date and title communicated.

**Salary on promotion:** handled per hr-confidential (move to at least the new band's minimum; standard increase if already within band; approval chain applies). Managers must not discuss band figures with members — direct compensation questions to HR/CM.

## 8. Step 3b — Compensation Linkage

ERIDU has three compensation levers, each connected to a different part of the biggieG loop. Rates, bands, and pool sizes live exclusively in hr-confidential; this section defines only how the mechanisms connect.

| Lever | Cadence | What it rewards | biggieG connection |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Incentive** | Monthly (in payroll) | Ongoing variable-cost optimization or revenue growth, as a proportion of value created, tied to measurable KPIs | Independent of initiatives — but incentive KPIs and initiative metrics should point at the same pillar metrics so daily work and quarterly development reinforce each other. Formula transparent to the employee. |
| **Bonus** | Quarterly | One-time project success / achievement of assigned initiatives (which ladder into the quarter's QSGs) | **Directly driven by initiative assessment.** Bonus proposals are based on initiative scores from the Step-4 assessment. Bonus is earned, not guaranteed; each quarter evaluated independently; PIP members ineligible during PIP; mid-quarter joiners pro-rated. Proposed by Head of Pillar, approved by CM (Founder for Head-level). Paid within 1 month of assessment. |
| **Salary (promotion)** | On promotion / annual review | Sustained demonstration of next-level capability | Driven by the Skill Map readiness definition in Section 7. Bands reviewed annually by Founder + CM. |

**Initiative scoring for bonus purposes:**

| Score | Definition | Bonus signal |
|---|---|---|
| **Exceeded** | Success criteria surpassed; metric impact beyond target | Strong contribution to allocation |
| **Achieved** | All success criteria met | Standard contribution |
| **Partial** | Meaningful progress, criteria not fully met | Reduced; document why (member-controllable vs external) |
| **Missed** | Criteria not met for member-controllable reasons | No bonus contribution from this initiative; feeds next quarter's development plan or PIP consideration |

Scores are proposed by the manager with evidence, calibrated by HR, and approved through the standard chain. A "Partial" caused by an approved mid-quarter reprioritization is assessed against the adjusted goal, not the original.

## 9. Roles & Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibilities in biggieG |
|---|---|
| **Team member** | Self-assess honestly; commit to initiatives; raise blockers early; keep evidence of delivered work |
| **Manager (L2/L3)** | Assess with evidence; design initiatives against gaps and metrics; coach through the quarter; run mid-quarter checkpoint; propose scores and promotion cases |
| **Head of Pillar** | Approve initiatives and scores; identify promotion candidates; ensure the team's initiative portfolio covers the pillar's QSGs and metric targets |
| **HR** | Calibrate ratings and scores across pillars; maintain records; track promotion pipeline and probation/PIP interactions; run the cycle calendar; guard confidentiality boundaries |
| **Country Manager** | Approve promotions, bonuses, incentive changes; approve Head-level initiatives; review the framework quarterly |
| **Founder** | Approve Head-level promotions and bonuses; approve changes to bands, pools, rates (per hr-confidential) |

**Interaction with PIP:** biggieG is a development framework, not a substitute for performance management. Sustained "Below level" ratings on core categories may trigger a PIP per hr-ops. PIP members remain in the assessment cycle but are bonus-ineligible during the PIP period, and their initiatives become the PIP improvement goals.
