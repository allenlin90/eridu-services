# HR Policies And Benefits

The employee-facing half of ERIDU's HR policy — what every employee is entitled to and how HR processes work day to day. For manager-only process guidance (performance improvement conversations), see [[hr.hr-management-processes]].

## What HR covers

HR is an operational support function serving all three pillars and company-wide. It handles recruiting, onboarding, payroll preparation, employee records, compliance with Thai labor law, and access management. Team: a dedicated internal HR person, reporting to the Country Manager.

**Key legal framework:** Thai Labor Protection Act B.E. 2541 (1998) and amendments including LPA Amendment No. 9 B.E. 2568 (2025). Companies with 10+ employees must maintain written work rules in Thai, displayed at the workplace and submitted to the local labor office.

## Employment types

| Type | Contract | Social Security | Withholding tax | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Full-time employee | Employment contract | SSO registered (Section 33) | PND 1 (payroll WHT) | Standard labor law protections apply |
| Part-time employee | Employment contract (part-time terms) | SSO registered if eligible | PND 1 | Same labor protections, pro-rated benefits |
| Contractor (MC, creator, freelancer) | Service contract | Not SSO registered | PND 3 (individual) | Not covered by Labor Protection Act — governed by contract terms |
| Intern | Internship agreement | Not SSO registered | PND 1 if paid | Limited scope, HR recruits and decides |

## Onboarding and probation

**Onboarding checklist:** signed contract, national ID and tax documents, SSO registration (employees, within 30 days), bank details for payroll, emergency contact, system access provisioned, employee handbook acknowledged, brand/confidentiality briefing, team introduction, assigned onboarding buddy or manager.

**Probation:** contract probation is 119 days (Thai law standard — avoids severance obligation at 120 days); internal management evaluation runs 90 days. Probationary employees are still protected by the Labor Protection Act.

- Day 30: first check-in — initial task performance, cultural fit, attendance, documented feedback.
- Day 60: mid-probation review — progress against tasks and objectives, issues flagged early.
- Day 90: final evaluation — formal pass/fail decision.
- Outcomes: **pass** (confirmed permanent), **conditional pass** (specific improvement areas, extended to max Day 119), or **fail** (termination before Day 120, no severance required).

## Promotion, bonus, and incentives

**Incentives (monthly):** tied to variable cost optimization or revenue growth, calculated as a proportion of value created, paid monthly with payroll. Targets set by Head of Pillar, approved by Country Manager.

**Bonus (quarterly):** tied to one-time project success or quarterly strategic goals, assessed independently each quarter, criteria set at the start of the quarter. Approved by Head of Pillar + Country Manager.

**Promotion:** tied to consistent demonstration of the next level's skillset and ownership — not tenure or current-role performance alone. The employee must already be operating at the next level, not be promoted in hopes they'll grow into it. Evaluation covers technical/functional skills, end-to-end ownership, decision-making quality, leadership (if applicable), and values alignment. Decided by Head of Pillar, approved by Country Manager, with Founder approval required for Head-level promotions. Reviewed quarterly but can be initiated any time criteria are clearly met.

## Termination and severance

Thai Labor Protection Act requirements apply to every termination:

- Written notice at least one pay period in advance, or payment in lieu of notice.
- Severance by length of service: under 120 days — none; 120 days–1 year — 30 days' wages; 1–3 years — 90 days' wages; 3–6 years — 180 days' wages; 6–10 years — 240 days' wages; 10–20 years — 300 days' wages; 20+ years — 400 days' wages.
- Final pay (salary, overtime, unused annual leave) paid within 3 days of termination.
- No severance required for: dishonesty, intentional criminal offense, gross negligence causing serious damage, violation of work rules after a written warning (within 1 year), abandonment of duty for 3 consecutive working days, or imprisonment.

## Communication and grievances

- HR communications: LINE for quick updates, email for formal/documented matters. Policy changes are always communicated in writing via email and acknowledged.
- Grievances: raise with your direct manager, HR, or the Country Manager. Investigated within 7 days, resolved or escalated within 14 days, outcome documented.
- All HR decisions (warnings, PIP, termination, promotions) are documented in writing.

## Working hours and leave

**Working hours:** office staff work fixed 8-hour days, not exceeding 48 hours/week. Studio/production staff (Erify) work shift-based schedules. Minimum 1 hour rest after 5 consecutive hours of work. Overtime requires prior consent, capped at 36 hours/week, paid at 1.5x (working days), 2x (holidays), or 3x (overtime on holidays).

**Leave entitlements** (per LPA and the 2025 Amendment No. 9, effective 7 December 2025):

| Leave type | Entitlement | Pay | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Annual leave | 6 working days/year after 1 year of continuous service | Full pay | No statutory entitlement before 1 year |
| Sick leave | Unlimited days as necessary | Employer pays max 30 days/year | Medical certificate required for 3+ consecutive days |
| Maternity leave | 120 days per pregnancy (including holidays) | Employer pays up to 60 days' wages; SSO covers additional days | Includes prenatal and postnatal |
| Spousal/paternity leave | 15 days | Full pay | Must be taken within 90 days of birth |
| Childcare leave (newborn) | 15 consecutive days | 50% pay | For female employees when newborn has medical complications; requires medical certificate |
| Public holidays | Minimum 13 days/year | Full pay | As announced by government annually |
| Weekly holiday | Minimum 1 day/week | N/A | Typically Sunday; max 6 days between days off |
| Ordination leave | Up to 30 days (company policy) | Per company policy | For male Buddhist employees entering monkhood |
| Military service leave | Up to 60 days/year | Full pay | Per Thai law |
| Sterilization leave | As advised by doctor | Full pay | Per Thai law |

**Leave process:** submit requests to your direct manager before the leave period (sick leave may be notified same-day). Coordinate annual leave with your team for coverage. Sick leave of 3+ days needs a medical certificate on return. Give HR as much advance notice as possible for maternity leave. All leave is tracked by HR.

**Attendance:** tracked daily. Late arrivals/early departures must be reported to your direct manager. "Late" means arriving more than 15 minutes after your scheduled start. 3 consecutive unexcused absences is grounds for termination without severance.

## Recruiting

**Full-time:** org planning (Founder + Country Manager) → job requirements (Head of Pillar) → sourcing/screening (HR) → interview → hiring manager decision, subject to Country Manager approval → offer → onboarding.

**Interns:** HR sources, screens, interviews, and decides; Country Manager informed but approval not required.

**Contractors (MCs, freelancers):** pillar-specific — Erify Offset team manages MC recruitment, ERISA manages creator recruitment. HR ensures service contracts are signed and required documents collected before onboarding; no SSO registration for contractors.

## Payroll

|---|---|---|
| 1st–3rd | Collect attendance, leave, overtime, incentive data for previous month | HR |
| 3rd–5th | Calculate payroll: base salary, incentives, overtime, deductions | HR |

Payslips are issued to all employees. Payroll records are retained a minimum of 2 years after termination; tax records a minimum of 5 years.
