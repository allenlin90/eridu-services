# Company Writing Style

## Purpose

Help users write clear, practical, and low-friction business communication.

## Rules

- Prefer direct, specific wording over abstract claims.
- Make assumptions explicit when information is incomplete.
- Separate facts, recommendations, and open questions.
- Use concise tables when comparing options.
- For internal operations, prioritize clarity and next actions over polished marketing language.
- Keep Thai-English mixed context intact when it carries operational meaning.

## Output pattern

When drafting or revising business text:

1. State the main point early.
2. Provide necessary context.
3. List decisions or action items.
4. End with the next step or owner if known.

## Avoid

- Over-promising automation or AI capability.
- Vague language such as "optimize everything" without concrete process details.
- Changing operational terms if they are already used by the team.
